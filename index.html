About Rococo

Rococo, sometimes known as Late Baroque, is an ornamental and theatrical style of architecture, art and decoration. It combines asymmetry, scrolling curves, gilding, white and pastel colors, sculpted molding, and trompe l'oeil frescoes to create the illusion of motion and drama. It first appeared in France and Italy in the 1730s and spread to Central Europe in the 1750s and 1760s.

Popular Artists of Rococo Era

Jean-Honoré FRAGONARD

Jean-Honoré Fragonard, was born April 5, 1732 in France and died August 22, 1806, Paris. He was a painter whose most popular works, included The Swing (1767) which is characterized by delicate hedonism.
